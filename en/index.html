Japanese Calligraphy

I studied Japanese calligraphy at Nihon Shodou Kyoiku Gakkai school in Tokyo under the guidance of calligraphy masters Akiko Crowther and Kaori Ishijima. I achieved 7th dan in various calligraphy styles and disciplines and passed the Shi kyo exam, which qualifies me to teach.

 

I create custom calligraphy works and organize exhibitions of my original works and paintings in cultural institutions in the Czech Republic and around the world.

I also organize exhibitions of my students' works, which we arrange into thematic collections. I participate in events where I present Japanese calligraphy to the Czech public.

In the past, I regularly collaborated with Galerie Kritiků and the Czech Embassy in Tokyo on cultural exchange between the Czech Republic and Japan. As part of this collaboration, I prepared calligraphy works for annual exhibitions in Japan organized by Tokyo Shodó Geijutsu Gakkai - the Association of Oriental Calligraphic Art.

 

Awards

2021 and 2022 awards from the Metropolitan Museum in Tokyo – gold medal from Tokyo Shodó Geijutsu Gakkai (Association of Oriental Calligraphic Art).

Iroha Japanese Calligraphy School

 

 

In Prague, I founded the IROHA calligraphy school, which has about fifty students. I prepare some of them for exams for the Japanese calligraphy school in Tokyo.

I teach the traditional rinsho method, transcribing texts from old masters and templates from basic to abstract styles. At the same time, I guide my students towards creative and personal writing.
